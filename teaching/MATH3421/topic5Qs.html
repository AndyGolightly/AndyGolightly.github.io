Topic 5 Questions

Not for hand in. These are about as hard as it gets. Solutions are available here.

  1. Consider a weight function of the form \[w_t(x_t) = \frac{p(y_t|x_t)p(x_t|x_{t-1})}{g(x_t|x_{t-1})}.\]

    (a) Show that \[\mathrm{Var}_g[w_t(X_t)]=\left(\int \frac{[p(x_t|x_{t-1})p(y_t|x_t)]^2}{g(x_t|x_{t-1})}dx_t \right) - [p(y_t|x_{t-1})]^2.\] Hint: Recall that for a random variable \(X\), \(\mathrm{Var}(X)=\mathbb{E}(X^2)-[\mathbb{E}(X)]^2\).

    (b) Suppose that \(g(x_t|x_{t-1})=p(x_t|x_{t-1},y_t)\). Note that explicit conditioning of the density \(g\) on \(y_t\) has been supressed for notational simplicity. Show that \(\mathrm{Var}_g(w_t(X_t))=0\).

  2. Consider a hidden Markov model of the form \[\begin{aligned} X_0&\sim \textrm{Uniform}\{0,1\}\\ X_t|x_{t-1}&\overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim} (1-\epsilon)\delta (x_t-x_{t-1}) + \epsilon \delta (x_t-[1-x_{t-1}])\\ Y_t|x_t&\overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim} N(x_t\,,\,1)\end{aligned}\] for \(t=0,\ldots,T\). Note that \(X_t\) has a discrete state space: it only takes values \(0\) or \(1\).

    (a) Briefly describe the steps for simulating values of \(Y_t\) at \(t=0,1,\ldots,T\).

    (b) Find the optimal importance proposal kernel \(p(x_t|x_{t-1},y_t)\).

    (c) Suppose that \(x_{t-1}=0\). How large does \(y_t\) need to be in order for half of the proposed values from \(p(x_t|x_{t-1},y_t)\) to be 1? Give your answer in terms of \(\epsilon\).

  3. Consider a hidden Markov model of the form \[\begin{aligned} X_0 &\sim N(0,1)\\ X_t|x_{t-1} &\overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim} N(x_{t-1}\,,\,1)\\ Y_t|x_t &\overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim} N(x_t\,,\,1)\end{aligned}\] for \(t=0,\ldots,T\). Suppose that \(X_{t-1}|y_{0:t-1}\sim N(m_{t-1}, V_{t-1})\) where the mean \(m_{t-1}\) and variance \(V_{t-1}\) are some functions of \(y_{0:t-1}\).

    (a) Find the distrubution of \(X_{t}|y_{0:t-1}\).

    (b) Find the distribution of the random vector \((X_t,Y_t)'\) conditional on \(y_{0:t-1}\).

    (c) Hence find the distribution of \(X_t|y_{0:t}\).
    Hint: If \[\begin{pmatrix}X \\Y \end{pmatrix} \sim N\left\{ \begin{pmatrix} \mu_x \\ \mu_y \end{pmatrix}\,,\, \begin{pmatrix} \Sigma_{xx} & \Sigma_{xy} \\ \Sigma_{yx} & \Sigma_{yy} \end{pmatrix} \right\}\] then \(X|Y=y \sim N(\mu_{x|y},\Sigma_{x|y})\) where \[\begin{aligned} \mu_{x|y} &= \mu_x +\Sigma_{xy}\Sigma_{yy}^{-1}(y-\mu_y)\\ \Sigma_{x|y} &= \Sigma_{xx}-\Sigma_{xy}\Sigma_{yy}^{-1}\Sigma_{yx}.\end{aligned}\]
