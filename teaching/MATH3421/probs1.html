Week 3 Problems Class

For discussion at the Week 3 problems class, the following questions combine several elements from chapter 1 of the notes.

Preliminaries

  1. Suppose that \(X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)\). Let \(Y=\exp(X)\). Derive the probability density function (pdf) of the random variable \(Y\).
    (Note that \(Y\) follows a lognormal distribution and we write \(Y\sim logN(\mu,\sigma^2)\).)

  2. Consider two continuous random variables \(X\) and \(Y\) with joint pdf \(f_{X,Y}(x,y)\). Suppose that we can simulate from \(f_{X}(x)\) and \(f_{Y|X}(y|x)\) but we want to simulate from the marginal for \(Y\), \(f_{Y}(y)\). Consider a strategy that first draws \(x\) from \(f_{X}(x)\) and then \(\tilde{y}\) from \(f_{Y|X}(y|x)\). Show that the corresponding random variable \(\tilde{Y}\) has the same distribution as \(Y\).
    Hint: show that \(f_{\tilde{Y}}(\tilde{y})=f_Y(\tilde{y})\).

Crude oil volatility analysis

Consider the following data \(\boldsymbol{y}=(y_0,\ldots,y_{25})'\) where \(y_t\) is the closing price of crude oil (in USD/barrel) on day \(t\). Note that \(t=0\) corresponds to July 1st, 2022 and \(t=25\) corresponds to August 8th, 2022.

Crude oil closing prices (USD/barrel).
Day (\(t\)) Price (\(y_{t}\))
0 108.43
1 99.50
2 98.53
\(\vdots\) \(\vdots\)
24 89.01
25 88.35

Let \(X_t=\log Y_t\), \(t=0,\ldots,n\) and suppose we adopt a model of the form \[\begin{aligned} X_0 &\sim p(x_0),\\ X_t&=X_{t-1}+\epsilon_t, \quad \epsilon_t \overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim} N(0,\sigma^2), \quad t=1,\ldots,n.\end{aligned}\]

  1. Derive the transition density \(p(y_t|y_{t-1},\sigma)\).

  2. Briefly explain how you would simulate a single realisation \(\{Y_t\}_{t=0}^{n}\) from the model. Do not write any code.

  3. You are given that \(\sum_{t=1}^{25} [\log (y_t/y_{t-1})]^2 = 0.0305\). Let \(\tau=1/\sigma^{2}\) and suppose \(\tau\sim Ga(10,0.01)\) a priori. Derive the posterior distribution of \(\tau\). You may assume that the density of \(X_0\) (and therefore \(Y_0\)) does not depend on \(\tau\).

  4. Hence write down the posterior distribution of \(\sigma^2\).

  5. The posterior predictive distribution of \(Y_{26}\) has pdf given by \[\pi(y_{26}|\boldsymbol{y})=\int_{0}^{\infty} p(y_{26}|y_{25},\tau)\pi(\tau|\boldsymbol{y})d\tau.\] By appealing to the technique given in part 2., describe an algorithm for generating iid samples \(y_{26}^{(1)},\ldots,y_{26}^{(N)}\) from the predictive distribution of \(Y_{26}\).

  6. How could your approach in part 5. be extended to generate samples from the predictive distribution of \(Y_T\) where \(T>25\)?

  7. Suppose you have iid samples \(Y_T^{(1)},\ldots,Y_T^{(N)}\) from the predictive distribution of \(Y_T\), \(T>25\).

    (a) Write down a Monte Carlo estimator of \(p_T:=\mathbb{P}(Y_T > 88.35)\).

    (Note that the dependence of this probability on \(\boldsymbol{y}\) has been suppressed for notational simplicity.)

    (b) Write down an expression for the variance of the estimator in (a), leaving your answer in terms of \(N\) and \(p_T\).
