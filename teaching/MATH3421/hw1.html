Formative problems 1

  1. Beta particles are emitted independently from a nuclear source. Let \(X_i\) denote the time between the emission of the \((i-1)\)th and \(i\)th particle (\(i=1,2,\ldots)\) in minutes. The density of each \(X_i\) is given by \[f_{1}(x|\theta)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \theta\exp\{-\theta x\} & x>0,\, \theta>0,\\ 0 & \textrm{otherwise}. \end{array}\right.\] When you first obtain the data you find that you have only been given the number \(y_j\) of emissions in the interval \((j-1,j]\). You know, however, that from the above, \(Y_j\) (\(j=1,2,\ldots\)) are independent with the following mass function, \[f_{2}(y|\theta)=\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \frac{\theta^y}{y!}\exp\{-\theta\} & y=0,1,2,\ldots \, \theta>0,\\ 0 & \textrm{otherwise}. \end{array}\right.\] The last observation arrives exactly on the \(n\)th minute. Your prior distribution for \(\theta\) is a \(Gamma(a,b)\), with density \[\pi(\theta)\propto \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \theta^{a-1}\exp\{-b\theta\} & \theta,a,b>0,\\ 0 & \textrm{otherwise}. \end{array}\right.\]

    (a) Find the posterior distribution for \(\theta\) using \(y_1,\ldots,y_n\).

    (b) Find the posterior distribution for \(\theta\) using \(x_1,\ldots,x_m\) where \(m\) is the total number of particle emissions.

    (c) Explain why the two analyses give the same inferences on \(\theta\).

    (d) If \(a>1\), express the posterior mode of \(\theta\) as a weighted average of \(\boldsymbol{y}\) and the prior mode (where the weight \(\rho\) depends only on \(b/n\)).

    [15 marks]

  2. Describe a Monte Carlo algorithm to calculate the normalising constant \(f(\boldsymbol{x})\) in a Bayesian analysis. You should assume that the prior distribution \(\pi(\theta)\) is easy to directly simulate from.

    [5 marks]

  3. Describe a weighted resampling algorithm for generating draws from a posterior \(\pi(\theta|\boldsymbol{x})\) (known up to proportionality) using a proposal with density \(\pi(\theta)\), i.e. the prior. What are the weights? How can the weights be used to estimate the unknown normalising constant \(f(\boldsymbol{x})\)?

    [8 marks]

  4. Recall the detailed balance equation for a continuous state-space Markov chain: \[\pi(x)p(y|x)=\pi(y)p(x|y)\quad\forall x, y\in \mathcal{X}.\] By integrating both sides with respect to \(x\), show that \(\pi(\cdot)\) is a stationary distribution of the chain.

    [4 marks]

  5. Consider the AR(1) process: \[X_{n+1} = \alpha X_{n}+\epsilon_{n+1}, \quad\epsilon_{n+1}\overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim} N(0,\sigma^2), \quad |\alpha|<1.\]

    (a) Find the stationary mean and variance. Hint: in equilibrium, \(\mathbb{E}(X_{n+1})=\mathbb{E}(X_n)\) and \(\mathrm{Var}(X_{n+1})=\mathrm{Var}(X_n)\).

    (b) Suppose that \(\pi(x)\) is taken to be a Gaussian density with mean and variance from (a). Show that detailed balance is satisfied for this \(\pi(x)\). You may assume that the stationary distribution is Gaussian with mean and variance to be determined.

    [10 marks]

  6. Consider a random process: \[X_{n+1} = \alpha X_{n}+\epsilon_{n+1}, \quad \epsilon_{n+1}\overset{\textrm{indep}}{\sim}\textrm{Exp}(\lambda), \quad\alpha\in(0,1),\]

    (a) What is the transition kernel \(p(y|x)\) of the chain?

    (b) Given \(X_0=1\), describe an algorithm for simulating this chain up to step \(N\).

    [8 marks]
